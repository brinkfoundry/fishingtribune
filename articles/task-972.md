## How to Choose Fishing Gloves: What Actually Matters

**Temperature and Season**
This is the primary sorting variable. Below 40°F, you need neoprene or insulated fleece. Between 40–60°F, a lightweight fleece or neoprene convertible works. Above 60°F, you're in sun-protection territory — UPF fabric is the priority, warmth is irrelevant or actively unwanted.

**Dexterity Requirements**
If you're fly fishing, jigging with small lures, or tying a lot of knots, dexterity matters more than warmth. A convertible or fingerless design is usually worth the trade-off. If you're trolling, bottom fishing, or doing long fights with heavy gear, full-finger gloves work fine.

**Freshwater vs. Saltwater**
Saltwater is corrosive. Materials that degrade in salt water — some cheaper elastics, certain dyes, non-stainless hardware — will fail faster than the manufacturer claims. For saltwater, prioritize gloves marketed specifically for saltwater applications.
