## What to Look For When Buying a Digital Fishing Scale

**Accuracy rating:** Tournament fishing needs ±0.05 to ±0.1 pound. Recreational fishing is fine with ±0.2 pound. Don't pay for tournament accuracy if you're just keeping catfish for the fryer.

**Capacity:** Match capacity to your target species. Bass anglers need 30-50 pounds. Catfish and offshore anglers should look at 110-pound capacity models.

**Waterproofing:** IP ratings matter. IPX4 = splash resistant. IPX5-6 = water jet resistant. IPX7 = submersible to 1 meter. If you fish in rain or rough conditions, spend the extra few dollars for a real IP rating.

**Auto-hold feature:** A reading lock that holds the peak weight for 5 seconds is essential if you're fishing solo. Without it, you need four hands: two for the fish, one for the scale, one to see the display.

**Display:** Backlit is non-negotiable if you fish before sunrise or after sunset. Big LCD beats small display every time for quick reading.

**Hook vs. jaw grip:** Stainless hooks are versatile and work on all species. Jaw grips are gentler for lip-hooked bass but awkward on pike, catfish, and panfish.

**Battery life:** Most digital scales use CR2032 or AA batteries. Check what's included and how accessible the battery compartment is when wet.
