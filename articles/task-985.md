## What to Look for in a Fillet Knife

**Blade flexibility:** This is the single most important variable. Ultra-flexible blades for panfish and small trout — you need the tip to follow tight curves around ribs. Semi-rigid blades for fish over 3 lbs where you need to maintain blade direction over longer cuts.

**Blade length:** 6-7 inches for panfish and trout. 7.5-9 inches for walleye, bass, redfish. 9+ inches for salmon, pike, and large stripers. Using the wrong length is why people leave meat on the frame.

**Steel quality:** High-carbon stainless is the baseline. It holds an edge reasonably well and resists corrosion. German steels like X50CrMoV15 hold edges better but cost more. Avoid knives that only list "stainless" without further detail — that usually means cheap 420J2 steel that dulls after a handful of fish.

**Handle grip:** Your hands will be wet, slimy, and possibly cold.
