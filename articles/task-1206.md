## What to Look for in a Fishing Drone

Before we get into picks, you need to know what specs actually matter on the water — because drone specs sheets are written by marketing people, not anglers.

**Flight time** is the big one. Manufacturers test in calm conditions at moderate altitude. Real-world flight time in coastal wind with a payload drops fast. If the spec sheet says 30 minutes, budget 18-22 in real fishing conditions.

**Wind resistance** is rated in Beaufort scale levels. Level 5 (about 19-24 mph) is the minimum I'd consider for surf fishing. Level 6 (24-31 mph) is where you want to be for ocean work. Anything below that and you're grounded on half your fishing days.

**Payload capacity** matters if you're doing bait drops. Most surf fishing scenarios need at least 1-1.5 lb of capacity to get a decent rig out with a mullet or cut bait. Bigger baits for shark or large drum means you want 2+ lb.

**GPS stability and return-to-home** aren't optional. If you lose signal over water, you want that drone coming back to you, not ditching in the surf.

**Camera quality** matters for scouting even if you don't care about filming. You need to see structure, bait schools, and color changes in the water. 4K at 60fps is standard now on anything worth buying.

## Accessories Worth Adding

**Extra batteries** — This is non-negotiable. One battery isn't a fishing drone setup, it's a demonstration. Get at least two, preferably three. Budget $100-200 per extra battery depending on your drone model.

**Charging hub** — Multi-battery charging hubs let you cycle batteries efficiently between sessions. DJI's hubs are excellent; third-party options exist for other brands.

**Polarized sunglasses** — Sounds unrelated, but keeping your eyes on your drone and the water simultaneously is easier with quality polarized lenses reducing glare. Costa Del Mar 580G lenses are what I fish with.

**ND filter kit** — Reduces glare on water surface in the drone camera, dramatically improving your ability to see underwater structure and fish. Essential for scouting use. DJI's kits run $50-80.

**Rod holders and release accessories** — Gannet and SwellPro both sell accessory systems for their drones. Third-party payload release systems are available for DJI models on Amazon, ranging $40-120.

**Waterproof carrying case** — Protect your investment. Pelican cases are the gold standard. A 1510 carry-on size fits most drones with accessories.
