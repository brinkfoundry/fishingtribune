## What to Look for in a Fish Finder Under $500

**Screen size and resolution:** Bigger isn't always better — a kayak angler has different needs than someone running a 19-foot bass boat. 5-inch units work well on small vessels. 7-inch is the sweet spot for most situations. 9-inch is great if you have the dash space and want to run multiple views simultaneously.

**Sonar type:** Standard 2D CHIRP is the baseline. Down imaging (called DownScan, ClearVü, or Down Imaging depending on the brand) shows what's directly below in near-photographic detail. Side imaging (SideVü, SideScan) shows what's on either side of your boat — a major advantage for finding structure efficiently. The more sonar modes you have, the better you can read water.
