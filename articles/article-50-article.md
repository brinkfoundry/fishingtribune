## What Makes a Great Surf Rod?

Before we get into picks, let me tell you what I'm actually looking at when I grab a surf rod off the rack.

**Length:** Most surf rods run 9–15 feet. Longer means more leverage on the cast and more line clearance over breaking waves. A 12-footer is the sweet spot for most conditions. Go longer — 14 to 15 feet — when you're throwing heavy bait rigs into heavy surf. Go shorter — 9 to 10 feet — for light lure work in calm conditions or jetty fishing where overhead space is tight.

**Power:** Medium rods handle smaller fish and lighter rigs (pompano, whiting, surf perch). Heavy rods are built for drum, striped bass, sharks, and anything else that might actually test you. Most anglers do fine with a medium-heavy or heavy rod.

**Action:** Fast action rods load near the tip — better sensitivity, better hook sets. Moderate action loads lower in the blank — better for throwing heavy bait rigs because the deeper bend helps load the cast without snapping off your pyramid sinker and bait into the next county.

**Guides:** Salt is brutal on guides. Look for aluminum oxide or titanium frame guides, not chrome-plated steel. Fuji guides are the gold standard. Corroded guides destroy braid and mono faster than you'd believe.

**Handle:** Two-piece handles with a fore grip give you a place to brace the rod during a big cast. EVA foam holds up to salt, sand, and being dropped on the tailgate repeatedly.
