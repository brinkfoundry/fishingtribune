## What to Look for in an Ice Fishing Shelter

**Fabric Density:** Measured in denier — higher denier means thicker, warmer fabric. Budget shelters run 150-300D; premium shelters run 600D and above. If you fish below zero regularly, pay for the fabric.

**Hub System vs. Flip-Over:** Hubs are more versatile and work for any group size. Flip-overs are warmer and more self-contained but require a vehicle to tow. Most anglers who start with a flip-over eventually add a hub for group trips.

**Setup Time:** On a cold day with cold hands, the difference between a 90-second setup and a 10-minute setup is real. Prioritize hub systems if you move locations frequently.

**Floor Type:** Sewn-in thermal floors are warmer and keep wind from cutting under the shelter. Removable floors are more flexible. No floor means lighter weight and more portability.

**Ventilation:** Any shelter running a propane heater needs ventilation. Look for mesh windows with zippered covers rather than shelters that require you to crack a door.

**Weight vs. Capacity:** A rough rule — expect to carry about 3-5 pounds per person of capacity in a quality hub shelter. If a shelter claims 6-person capacity at 15 pounds, look hard at the fabric spec.
