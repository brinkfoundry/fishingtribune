## What to Look For in Fishing Rain Gear

**Waterproof Rating (Hydrostatic Head)**
The number in millimeters tells you how much water pressure the fabric can resist before it leaks. 10,000mm handles moderate fishing conditions. 20,000mm is the serious end. Anything below 5,000mm is light use only.

**Breathability**
Most rain gear rates breathability in g/m²/24hr — how many grams of moisture vapor pass through a square meter of fabric in 24 hours. Higher is better. Non-breathable gear like PVC works by just blocking everything, which means you stay damp from sweat if you're active.
