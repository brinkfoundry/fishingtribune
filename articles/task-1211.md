## What to Look for in a Fishing Cooler Bag

### Insulation Thickness
The single biggest predictor of ice retention is how much closed-cell foam is in the walls, lid, and base. Look for at least 1.5 inches of insulation for serious performance. Budget bags typically run 0.5–0.75 inches — adequate for a few hours, not a full day in summer heat.

### Zipper Quality
This is where cheap bags fail first. Look for #10 nylon coil zippers or RF-welded closures. Avoid thin plastic zippers on any bag you intend to use hard. If the zipper specs aren't published, that's a yellow flag.

### Liner Material
A TPU liner is leakproof and easy to clean. Fabric-lined interiors absorb fish odors and eventually grow mold. Non-negotiable for fishing use: get a TPU or similar hard liner.

### Weight and Carry System
An empty bag that weighs more than 5 lbs is a burden on long carries. Check the shoulder strap padding — a bare webbing strap on a 30-lb loaded bag will destroy your shoulder in two hours. Several bags in this list benefit from aftermarket strap pads if the stock option is thin.

### Attachment Points
If you're fishing from a kayak, canoe, or small skiff, look for MOLLE webbing, D-rings, or dedicated lash points on the exterior. A bag that slides around your deck is a bag that eventually goes overboard.
