## FAQ

**Q: What test curve rod do I need for carp fishing?**

It depends on where and how you're fishing. For day-ticket lakes and most club waters under 80 acres, a 3lb TC rod is the right choice — enough power to handle leads up to 3.5 oz and fish up to 40 pounds without the blank being brutal on the fight. Step to 3.5lb TC when you're casting beyond 100 yards regularly or fishing very large, open pit venues. Avoid going below 2.75lb TC unless you're specifically fishing small commercial fisheries with light rigs.

**Q: Do I need to use boilies, or can I use other baits?**

Boilies are the dominant carp bait because they're durable, selective (smaller species struggle to eat them), and can be loaded with attractors that work at distance and over time.
