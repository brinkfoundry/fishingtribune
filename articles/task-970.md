## What to Look for in a Tackle Box

**Compartment configurability.** Adjustable dividers beat fixed compartments for most anglers because your tackle mix changes by season and by species. A box that can reconfigure from ten big slots to twenty small ones is worth more than one that's locked into whatever the factory decided.

**Material quality.** Look for thick-walled polypropylene construction on hard boxes. Thin plastic cracks in cold weather and warps in heat. On bags, 600D polyester is the minimum — 1000D or higher if you're hard on gear.

**Latch and hinge durability.** The lid mechanism is the first thing that fails on cheap tackle boxes. Stainless steel hardware or reinforced composite hinges last significantly longer than basic plastic components.

**Water resistance.** True waterproofing matters most for terminal tackle — hooks, swivels, split shot, and anything else that rusts. Gasket-sealed boxes like the Plano Edge are worth the premium if you're fishing wet environments.
