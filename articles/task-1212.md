**Price:** ~$2,999 | **Thrust:** 112 lbs | **Shaft Length:** 45, 52, or 60 inches | **Weight:** 36 lbs | **Power:** 24V

The Minn Kota Ultrex 112 is what happens when the two best technologies in trolling motors — Ulterra's auto-deploy and Terrova's i-Pilot GPS — get combined into a single unit. That Spot-Lock feature is worth dwelling on. When you're trying to stay on a specific piece of laydown or a transition point on a ledge, manually fighting wind and current while keeping a bait in a 10-foot zone is exhausting. Spot-Lock does it for you. You fish harder, you fish longer, and you hit the strike zone more consistently.

**Real-World Note:** At 36 lbs, this motor is heavy. Budget for a good mounting bracket and make sure your bow deck is reinforced. The auto-deploy is smooth but requires a properly tensioned spring — check it twice a season.

## FAQ: Bass Boat Accessories

**Q: What's the most important accessory upgrade for a bass boat?**
A: If you're fishing tournaments, it's the fishfinder or trolling motor — pick the gap in your current setup. If you're recreational fishing, start with a quality fishfinder upgrade. The ability to see structure changes everything about where you cast and how you present baits. A unit like the Garmin ECHOMAP Ultra 106sv or even the budget Humminbird HELIX 7 will show you fish and features that your old depth finder never could.

**Q: Is the Minn Kota Ultrex worth $3,000?**
A: For tournament anglers, yes — without question. For recreational fishing, it depends how much you value boat control. If you fish windy reservoirs, do a lot of deep structure fishing, or solo fish frequently, the Spot-Lock GPS anchoring alone is worth the investment. If you fish small ponds and slow rivers, a less expensive Minn Kota Terrova at around $1,500 covers most of the same ground.
