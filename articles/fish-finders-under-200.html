FAQ

Do I really need a fish finder?
No. But it tells you whether fish are present before you commit to working a spot. Think of it as eliminating the slowest part of scouting, not guaranteeing fish.
What's the difference between castable and mounted?
Mounted gives continuous real-time depth as you move. Castable transmits data when you cast to a specific spot. Mounted for boats, castable for shore/kayak/ice.
Will a cheap fish finder help me catch more?
If you're new to a body of water, yes — the information advantage is direct. The Helix 5 and Hook2 give you accurate data that translates to better decisions.
Is GPS necessary?
More useful than most expect. GPS lets you drop waypoints on productive spots and build a private map over a season. The Helix 5 G2 includes it under $200.
