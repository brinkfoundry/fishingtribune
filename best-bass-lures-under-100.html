There are few things in fishing as satisfying as a big bass blowing up on a topwater lure on a calm summer morning. The Zara Spook has been triggering that experience since 1939. It's not a coincidence that it's still the gold standard for walk-the-dog topwater presentations more than 80 years later.

The bait's action comes entirely from the angler — a rhythmic rod-tip-down twitch-twitch-pause cadence that causes the lure to walk side to side across the surface. Bass can't stand it. The presentation mimics a dying baitfish struggling at the surface, and when you get the cadence right, you'll understand why people get addicted to topwater fishing.

I've used the Spook on open-water structure, over hydrilla beds, and along dock edges. It shines most in low-light conditions — first and last hour of daylight — and on calm mornings when the surface is glassy. On choppy water, the smaller Spook Jr. (1/2 oz, 3.5 inches, ~$9) outperforms the original.
