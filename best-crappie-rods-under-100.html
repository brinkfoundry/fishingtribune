What to Look for in a Crappie Rod

Length:** For most crappie techniques, 6'6"–7' is the versatile sweet spot. Spider rigging or long-lining? Go 10–16 feet. Kayak and bank fishing in tight cover? Drop to 6'.

Power:** Ultra-light or light. Crappie average 1/4–3/4 lb. You need a rod that loads under that weight to telegraph bites. Medium or heavy power rods kill the tactile feedback you need.

Action:** Fast action for jigging — quick tip recovery and good sensitivity. Moderate-fast if you're slow trolling or want a more forgiving fight. Avoid extra-fast for crappie; you'll pull hooks.

Material:** Full graphite (IM6, IM7, 24-ton carbon) gives you better sensitivity. Fiberglass composite gives you durability. For most crappie fishing, composite is adequate; for finesse jigging in clear water, graphite makes a real difference.

Line Guides:** Zirconia or SiC inserts matter when running 4 lb fluorocarbon and 1/32 oz jigs. Cheap aluminum guides create friction that kills casting distance and can cut light line.
